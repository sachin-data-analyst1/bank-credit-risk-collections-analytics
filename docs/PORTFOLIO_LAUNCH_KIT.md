## 3. LEGITIMATE "ATS SHORTLISTING" TACTICS (no gimmicks — these actually work)

Real ATS systems (Workday, Greenhouse, Taleo, iCIMS) parse text, not tricks. What actually
moves the needle:

1. **Mirror exact phrasing from the job description.** If the JD says "stakeholder reporting,"
   use that exact phrase somewhere (not "reporting to stakeholders"). ATS keyword match is
   often literal substring matching.
2. **Use standard section headers**: "Work Experience," "Skills," "Education," "Projects" —
   not creative headers like "My Journey." Parsers look for these labels.
3. **Submit as .docx or a text-selectable PDF** — never an image-based PDF or a heavily
   graphic-designed template with columns/text boxes; many ATS mis-parse multi-column layouts.
4. **Avoid tables, headers/footers, and text boxes for content that must be read** — put
   everything in the main body flow.
5. **Spell out acronyms once** (e.g., "Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)") so you match both
   keyword variants.
6. **Quantify every bullet** — recruiters and hiring managers (the humans after the ATS)
   respond to numbers: "30,000 records," "4x default-rate spread," "zero formula errors."
7. **Put the GitHub/portfolio link at the top** near your name/contact info — recruiters
   check it within the first 10 seconds.
8. **Do NOT use hidden white text keyword stuffing.** Modern ATS (and any human who opens
   the file) can detect it; some systems flag it and it can get an application auto-rejected
   or reported. It's not a real hack — it actively hurts you.
